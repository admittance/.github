# admittance

> **Who you are, on your terms. Access without surrender.**

Identity online has been quietly inverted. To prove who you are, you hand yourself over — to a central login, a single sign-on, a handful of companies that become the gatekeepers between you and everywhere you go. Convenience was the bait; dependence was the price. The work gathered here is built on the opposite instinct: that you should be able to prove who you are and be let in, without surrendering yourself to a middleman to do it.

The focus is the front door — authentication and identity treated as infrastructure rather than as a service you rent. Access that lives at the edge, bound to the place you are actually visiting instead of pooled in a central hub. Identity you assert and control yourself, on open terms, rather than borrow from an account that can be revoked, mined, or held hostage. Strong, modern, phishing-resistant security as the floor for everyone, with no quiet exceptions.

The throughline is ownership. The right to be admitted should belong to the person being admitted — not to whoever happens to be holding the keys.
